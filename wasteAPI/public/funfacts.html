    • Fun facts

    GLOBAL FACT

    Americans make up roughly 5% of the world’s population, but generate nearly 40% of the world’s total waste.

    PEOPLE FACT

    If every garbage truck we filled (annually) was placed end-to-end, it would cover half the distance to the moon or ~119,450 miles.

    LANDFILL FACT

    Because landfills are so densely packed, much of the degradation that occurs is anaerobic (without air).

    FOOD WASTE FACT

     Roughly one-third of the food produced in the world for human consumption every year (approximately 1.3 billion tons) is wasted.

    WATER FACT

    Annually, more than 14 billion pounds of garbage is dumped into the world’s ocean (mainly marine life toxic plastic).

    E-WASTE FACT

    If all U.S. cell phones were recycled in a given year, it would save enough energy to power ~25,000 households for one year.

    GLOBAL FACT

    The only relatively “clean” place on Earth is Antarctica. It is protected by the Antarctic Treaty, which prohibits military activities, mineral mining, nuclear explosions and nuclear waste disposal.

    PEOPLE FACT

    Today, most communities are spending more on waste management than they are for schoolbooks, fire protection, libraries, and parks.

    LANDFILL FACT

    Over 60% of the trash that ends in dustbin could be recycled.

    FOOD WASTE FACT

    If one-fourth of the food currently lost or wasted globally could be saved, it would be enough to feed roughly 870 million people.

    WATER FACT

    Almost half of all seabird species, 22% of cetaceans, all sea turtle species, and a growing list of fish species have been documented with plastic found in or around their bodies.

    E-WASTE FACT

    When you recycle just one aluminum can, you save enough energy to power a 100-watt light bulb for 20 hours or to listen to a full album on your iPod.
